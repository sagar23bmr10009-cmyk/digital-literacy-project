Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

What the crime is: UPI fraud occurs when scammers trick victims into authorizing a money transfer from their bank account via a Unified Payments Interface (UPI) app.

How it typically happens: A common tactic involves the "QR Code Scam." A scammer poses as a buyer for an item a student listed online. The scammer sends a QR code to the student on WhatsApp, claiming that scanning it will receive the payment. When the student scans the code and enters their UPI PIN—believing they are authenticating the receipt of funds—money is actually deducted from their account and sent to the scammer.

Who is targeted: College students are frequent targets because they frequently use digital payments for splitting bills, buying second-hand books, or paying for college events, making them highly active on UPI platforms.

Consequences: Victims suffer immediate financial loss, which can be devastating for a student on a tight budget. It also leads to a loss of trust in digital payment systems and requires stressful, time-consuming processes involving the bank and cyber police to attempt to recover the funds.
